Finding Licensed Child Care in Toronto: A Guide for Parents

All about Licensed Child Care Centers

by Natalia Naranjo Barrera

Toronto offers a wide range of licensed child care centers designed to meet the diverse needs of families moving to the city. These centers, regulated by the Ontario Ministry of Education, provide safe and educational environments for children from infancy to age 12. Options include daycare programs, preschool services, and before- and after-school care, offering flexibility for working parents.

Comparison of Child Care Centers by Sponsorship Type and Government Subsidies

This analysis aims to compare the number of licensed child care centers in Toronto that receive government subsidies with those that do not, categorized by their type of sponsorship. We will differentiate between centers operated by commercial agencies, non-profit organizations, and public city-operated agencies. Understanding these distinctions can assist parents in making informed decisions about the child care options that best align with their needs and financial situations.

Conclusion

Understanding the distribution of licensed child care centers in Toronto is essential for parents seeking to secure quality care and education for their children. Our analysis suggests that it is generally easier to find a subsidized child care spot through a non-profit agency. Additionally, the four wards with the highest number of child care centers are Etobicoke-Lakeshore (Ward 3), Parkdale-High Park (Ward 4), Toronto-Danforth (Ward 14), and Beaches-East York (Ward 19).

When considering availability by age group, preschool-aged children have the highest chances of securing a spot, while finding care for infants tends to be more challenging. We recommend reviewing the final charts carefully to assess availability based on your child’s age and specific needs.
